## Design Concept Summary

**Concept: Iron & Gold**

Ironclad Roofing Co. presents as a luxury trade brand — the kind of roofer that a $1.2M homeowner calls. The aesthetic draws from Apple product pages and high-end residential architecture firms: near-black backgrounds, warm gold accents, generous whitespace, and full-bleed photography that does the selling.

The antithesis of everything in the local contractor category: no stock-photo handshakes, no navy-and-white clipart, no "Free Estimates!" banners. Instead: drama, confidence, and proof.

**Emotional target:** Visitor feels safe, protected, and certain they are hiring the best — not gambling on an unknown contractor.

## Hero Variant Descriptions

### Variant A — Centered Overlay (not selected)
- Full-bleed aerial photo of Austin rooftop at golden hour
- Headline centered over image in large serif font
- Thin horizontal gold rule line below headline
- Centered gold pill CTA
- Feels: editorial, magazine cover
- Drawback: centered hero is common, less differentiated

### Variant B — Left-Aligned Split (SELECTED — BUILD THIS)
- Left 50%: solid `#1C1C1E` background with text content
- Right 50%: full-bleed aerial photo, no overlay
- Short gold accent bar (`w-12 h-1 bg-gold`) above headline
- Headline: `display-xl`, ivory, left-aligned, Manrope 800
- Subheadline below, then gold pill CTA
- Feels: architectural magazine, premium editorial, completely unique in the roofing category
- Desktop breakpoint: side-by-side. Mobile: photo top (40vh), text below (60vh)

**Why Variant B:** In a market where every roofing site uses a full-bleed hero with centered text, a split-panel hero immediately signals "this company is different." The left dark panel makes the headline typography the hero, not just the photo. Higher perceived brand value.
